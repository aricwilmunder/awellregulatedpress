Mission

A Well-Regulated Press was established to support the discovery, preservation, interpretation, and sharing of historical stories through publishing, research, education, and public engagement.

The press grew from the work of The Prescott Girls Project, an ongoing effort inspired by the discovery of a collection of nineteenth-century family samplers and the historical investigations that followed. What began as artifact research expanded into educational resources, museum collaborations, public presentations, historical interpretation, and publishing initiatives designed to make historical knowledge accessible to broader audiences.

Artifacts are more than objects. They are evidence of lives lived, relationships formed, communities built, and stories remembered. Through careful research and responsible interpretation, A Well-Regulated Press seeks to reconnect artifacts, records, and historical places with the people behind them.

Our Principles

Preservation

Documenting and preserving artifacts, records, provenance, and historical context so that they remain available to future generations.

Research

Pursuing evidence-based historical investigation while remaining transparent about what is known, what is uncertain, and what remains to be discovered.

Interpretation

Transforming research into accessible narratives, visualizations, presentations, and educational experiences that help modern audiences connect with the past.

Education

Creating books, resources, and programs that encourage curiosity, historical inquiry, and lifelong learning.

Community

Building relationships among museums, libraries, educators, researchers, descendants, historical organizations, and readers who share an interest in preserving and understanding history.

Outreach

Ensuring that research, stories, educational resources, and public programs reach the audiences and institutions that can benefit from them.

The Prescott Girls Project

A Well-Regulated Press is one of several initiatives that support the broader mission of The Prescott Girls Project. The project exists to discover, preserve, interpret, and share the stories connected to the Prescott family, their samplers, and the people, places, and events that shaped their lives.

The project extends beyond any single book, article, presentation, or artifact. It includes historical and genealogical research, educational initiatives, museum collaborations, public-history presentations, historical interpretation, and the continuing exploration of the Prescott family story.

Current Initiatives

  • Prescott Family Research Collection

New discoveries, partnerships, publications, and educational opportunities are welcomed. While individual projects may evolve, the mission remains constant: to preserve, interpret, and share historical stories in ways that are accurate, engaging, and accessible to future generations.
